# Code of Conduct

## Purpose
This repository is maintained as a professional, respectful, and evidence-driven collaboration space.
We want contributors, reviewers, maintainers, and community members to feel safe participating in technical discussions without harassment, hostility, or unnecessary friction.

## Our Standards
Examples of behavior that help create a healthy environment include:

- Being respectful, constructive, and specific in issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions.
- Focusing feedback on code, architecture, evidence quality, documentation, tests, and product behavior rather than on people.
- Assuming good intent while still holding a high bar for correctness, traceability, and repository standards.
- Welcoming different experience levels and helping others learn the repository, tooling, and review expectations.
- Raising concerns early when you notice quality, security, privacy, or compliance risks.
- Respecting the public nature of the repository and communicating with care.

## Unacceptable Behavior
The following behavior is not acceptable in this project:

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, hate speech, or personal attacks.
- Trolling, baiting, repeated antagonistic behavior, or deliberately derailing technical discussion.
- Doxxing or exposing private, personal, tenant, customer, employee, or confidential business information.
- Sharing secrets, credentials, tokens, connection strings, internal-only documents, or private datasets in public issues, pull requests, comments, logs, screenshots, or generated artifacts.
- Misrepresenting evidence, hiding material context, or knowingly posting misleading technical claims.
- Spam, off-topic promotion, or repeated low-signal noise that disrupts collaboration.

## Collaboration Expectations
This project has a strong trust and traceability model. When participating, contributors are expected to:

- Keep discussions relevant to the repository and grounded in observable facts.
- Prefer reproducible evidence over speculation.
- Flag uncertainty clearly instead of overstating confidence.
- Respect maintainers' requests on scope, compliance, and release readiness.
- Avoid posting sensitive operational details in public threads when a private escalation path is more appropriate.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness conduct problems, report them privately to the maintainers through GitHub's private maintainer contact path when available.

For security-sensitive issues, secret exposure, or confidentiality concerns, do not open a public issue. Follow the repository security handling guidance in [docs/security/secrets-policy.md](./docs/security/secrets-policy.md) and use a private reporting path.

When reporting, include:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- Links or screenshots if relevant and safe to share
- Whether the issue involves harassment, confidentiality, or security risk

## Maintainer Responsibilities
Maintainers are responsible for protecting the health of the collaboration space. They may:

- Edit, hide, lock, or remove content that violates this Code of Conduct.
- Ask participants to pause, reframe, or move a discussion.
- Limit or revoke participation for repeated or severe violations.
- Escalate security or confidentiality incidents through the repository's applicable policies.

## Enforcement
Responses to violations depend on severity, pattern, and risk to people or the project. Actions may include:

1. A private warning or request to change behavior.
2. Removal of violating content or discussion access restrictions.
3. Temporary or permanent suspension from project spaces.

Serious harassment, threats, discrimination, or exposure of secrets or confidential data may lead to immediate removal without prior warning.

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- Issues
- Pull requests
- Code review comments
- Discussions
- Documentation feedback
- Shared screenshots, logs, attachments, and generated artifacts associated with repository collaboration

## Good-Faith Interpretation
Maintainers will interpret and enforce this policy in good faith, with the goal of protecting contributors and preserving productive collaboration.
Disagreement on technical direction is normal; disrespectful or unsafe conduct is not.
